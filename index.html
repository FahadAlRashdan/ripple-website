The problem

Global tools weren’t built to hear the Gulf

Your customers already tell you everything — in Kuwaiti dialect, in Arabizi, in emojis, in reviews. Most platforms simply can’t read it, so the most valuable feedback in your market goes unheard.

Arabic as an afterthought

Kuwaiti and Khaleeji dialect gets filed under Modern Standard Arabic. Right-to-left is a toggle, not a foundation — so meaning, sarcasm and slang are lost.

Dashboards, not decisions

Enterprise tools assume a full-time analyst reading charts. Most F&B teams don’t have one — and shouldn’t need one to know what to fix.

Benchmarks from elsewhere

Engagement norms drawn from markets nothing like Kuwait make good performance look bad — and real problems look fine.
