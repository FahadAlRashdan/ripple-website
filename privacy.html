Privacy Policy

What we collect, why, how long we keep it, and the choices you have — in plain language.

Last updated · 19 July 2026

The short version: Ripple reads public information only, never sells data, keeps each client's workspace isolated, publishes market intelligence as anonymous aggregates, and deletes raw data on a fixed schedule. Questions, anytime: Hello@RippleINT.ai.

1 · Who we are & what this covers

Ripple Intelligence ("Ripple", "we", "us") is an AI restaurant-intelligence platform for the food & beverage sector, operated from the State of Kuwait and serving Kuwait and the GCC. This policy explains what information we handle, why, how long we keep it, and the choices you have.

It covers two things: this website, and the Ripple platform — the subscription service our clients use. For anything this policy doesn't answer, write to us at Hello@RippleINT.ai.

2 · This website

This site has no user accounts, no advertising trackers, and no analytics cookies. It stores exactly one thing in your browser: your language preference (English or Arabic), kept locally on your device and sent to no one.

The contact form does not transmit anything to our servers — it opens your own email app with a pre-filled message, and we receive only what you choose to send. Our hosting provider processes standard technical request data (such as IP addresses) to serve the site securely.

3 · What the Ripple platform collects

Public content

The platform reads publicly available information only: public social media posts, captions and comments; public customer reviews; public creator content; and industry news and podcasts. It never accesses private accounts, private messages, or anything behind a login — and we never ask clients for their social media passwords.

Client-provided information

To set up a workspace, clients give us business information: brand handles, branch locations, the menu and its dialect aliases, a competitor watch-list, team contact details for report delivery, and brand-voice preferences. This lives in the client's isolated workspace.

Service records

We keep records generated by operating the service: the reports and alerts we produced, delivery logs, and technical logs used for debugging and security.

4 · How we use it

  • To deliver the service: analysis, scoring, alerts, reply drafts and reports for the subscribing client.
  • To improve dialect understanding: new Gulf-Arabic terms are mined from real public captions and approved by a person before entering our lexicon.
  • To support and bill our clients, and to keep the platform secure and prevent abuse.

We do not use client workspace data for advertising, and we do not sell data — to anyone, ever.

5 · AI processing

Ripple's analysis runs on enterprise AI services under commercial terms. Under those terms, content we submit for analysis is not used to train the providers' publicly available models. AI-drafted replies are always drafts: a person on the client's team reviews and sends every one.

One client's workspace data is never used to build features for, or shared with, another client. The only cross-client layer is market intelligence — and that is anonymised, as described next.

6 · Market intelligence stays anonymous

Market-level insights are published as cuisine- and area-level aggregates. No competitor brand or branch is ever named in shared market intelligence, and no individual reviewer or customer is profiled. Aggregates contain nothing that identifies a person.

7 · If you appear in public content

Ripple indexes public posts and reviews about restaurants. If you are an individual and would like a specific piece of your public content excluded from our processing, email Hello@RippleINT.ai with a link to it. We review every request promptly and in good faith.

9 · How long we keep data

  • Raw collected content (public posts, comments, reviews as gathered) — kept for up to 12 months, then deleted.
  • Derived aggregates (scores, indices, trends) — retained as the historical record that powers year-over-year analysis.
  • Operational logs — kept for up to 6 months for debugging and security, then deleted.

When a client leaves, their workspace — configuration, menu, contacts and brand data — is removed from live systems. Anonymised market-level aggregates, which contain nothing brand- or person-identifiable, persist as the market's historical record. Every exported report is watermarked with the client name and a timestamp.

10 · Security

Data is encrypted in transit and at rest. Each client workspace is isolated at the database layer with row-level security, administrative access is protected by multi-factor authentication, credentials are held in a managed vault, and access follows least-privilege principles.

11 · Your choices & rights

To ask what information we hold, or to request access, correction or removal, write to Hello@RippleINT.ai. We handle requests consistent with the laws of the State of Kuwait, including the Electronic Transactions Law No. 20 of 2014 and the data-privacy regulations issued by CITRA.

Ripple's services are directed at businesses, not children, and we do not knowingly collect children's personal information.
